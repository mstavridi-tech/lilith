# LILITH — Product Brief

**One-liner:** Every girl's pocket best friend: reads your chart to the T, tracks your cycle against the moon, talks to you like the friend who knows you best, and reminds you that you're not going crazy, you are the prize.

**Audience:** Women and gays, roughly 18 to 40, who are into astrology (from casual to obsessed), track or want to track their cycle, and want an app with a personality instead of a clinical tool. The woowoo delulu bad bitches.

**Positioning:** Co-Star has the depth but is cold and mean. The Pattern is the depth benchmark — its readings are so personal they're unsettling, and that "how does it KNOW me" effect is exactly what LILITH must achieve — but its tone is somber therapy-speak and its look is grey; we take the depth, not the vibe. Flo has the cycle science but is clinical. Stardust syncs periods to the moon but has no chart depth and no voice. ChatGPT listens but doesn't know her chart, her cycle, or her lore. LILITH is the only app that combines real natal chart depth, cycle and hormone awareness, an AI best friend with full context, and a voice that hypes her up but also tells her when it's not that deep. The voice and the context are the moat.

**The promise:** She should feel like the app understands her better than she understands herself. Every reading, affirmation, and chat reply draws on her actual placements, her actual cycle day, her actual week. Generic is the enemy.

## North star: the Best Friend Test

The vision in one sentence: **LILITH is every girl's best friend.** Not "an app with a friendly tone" — a best friend. Every feature, screen, notification, and sentence must pass the test: *would her best friend do this?*

A best friend:

- **Remembers.** Never asks your sign twice. Knows about the situationship, the boss, the mom era. Continuity is the product; amnesia is the failure
- **Shows up at the right moment,** not on a content calendar. Checks in on day 25 because she knows what day 25 means. Notices the full moon is in your sign before you do
- **Knows the lore.** References last week. "So how did the meeting actually go" energy
- **Hypes AND reality-checks.** Only gassing you up is a yes-man; only correcting you is your mother. A best friend does both, with love
- **Keeps secrets.** Everything told to LILITH is treated like a locked diary. Privacy isn't a settings page, it's loyalty
- **Never judges, never shames, never lectures.** Not about the ex, not about the third coffee, not about the 2am spiral
- **Talks like a person.** Never announcement voice, never brand voice, never "We've updated our features!"
- **Is there at 3am** and doesn't make it weird

The litmus for every design review: if a feature makes her feel *tracked*, it failed; if it makes her feel *known*, it passed. An app that asks for data is a form. A best friend who notices is magic.

## The core idea

Three data streams, one explanation of how you feel:

1. **The sky** — real planetary positions: transits, retrogrades, moon phase, eclipses
2. **Your chart** — your natal placements, computed from exact birth date, time, and place
3. **Your body** — cycle phase (and later, sleep and heart rate from Apple Health)

The app's job is to merge these into one daily answer to "why do I feel like this?" and one daily reminder that whatever the answer is, she's still that girl.

Example: *"You slept like trash and want to bite someone. Quick audit: you're day 24 (luteal, so progesterone is doing the most), the moon is full in YOUR sign, and Mercury just went retrograde. You're not unhinged, you're literally under cosmic and hormonal crossfire. Cancel something. You don't owe anyone an explanation."*

## Features by phase

### Phase 1 — MVP (what we're building now)
- **Onboarding:** account first, but the easiest account on iPhone: one tap on Sign in with Apple (FaceID, zero typing) gives us her name and email. Then her name confirmation ("what do we call you"), birth date, time, place. Whole flow under 90 seconds. The account holds identity and subscription; her cycle data and chat secrets stay on-device (Best Friend Test: a best friend knows your name AND keeps your diary locked)
- **Natal chart:** full chart with all planets, houses, rising sign, and Black Moon Lilith (our namesake placement gets special treatment)
- **Chart reading:** plain-language personality breakdown per placement. No jargon walls, every term explained like a friend would
- **Daily, weekly, and monthly horoscopes:** AI-written from her real chart + the real sky, in the LILITH voice. Weekly drops Sunday evening (planning her week is the ritual)
- **Moon view:** today's moon phase and sign, what it means for her chart
- **Personal affirmation notification:** one push a day, generated from HER chart and current transits. Never generic. "You are enough" is banned; "Your Aries Mars didn't come this far to be shy in that meeting" is the bar

### Phase 2 — The body and the cards
- Period logging and cycle phase detection (manual entry first)
- Apple Health integration: pull cycle data if she already tracks elsewhere, plus sleep and heart rate
- The merged daily insight (sky + chart + body), which is the killer feature
- **Hormone literacy (Maria's mission, June 11):** the daily "hormone weather report" by cycle phase, in girl: what estrogen, progesterone, and cortisol are likely doing and how that typically feels. Education, never diagnosis. Every insight carries the quiet bridge: "if this feels extreme every month, that's worth a real doctor conversation, here's what to ask for." The app that tells her she may not be crazy, her estrogen may just be wrecked, and helps her find out for real
- Luteal-phase affirmation mode (extra gentle, extra hype)
- **Tarot:** daily card pull as ritual, beautiful deck in the house style, AI interpretation that ties the card to her chart and her day. Optional 3-card spreads for specific questions

### Phase 2.5 — The friend who remembers your health admin
- **Care reminders:** age-based screening nudges in voice: Pap smears, mammograms, general checkups. Based on published public-health guidelines, adjustable by country and personal schedule, always framed as "book it with your provider", never as medical advice. The best friend who remembers the appointment you keep forgetting to make
- Manual logging of her own lab results later (she types in what her doctor gave her; the app helps her track it over time, never interprets diagnostically)

### Phase 5 — The fertility companion (after the core is loved)
- **Egg freezing / IVF cycle organizer:** her clinic's calendar, appointment and injection reminders she enters herself, costs tracker, and the voice holding her hand through a hard process. Organizer and emotional support, never dosing advice, never fertility predictions

### Phase 3 — The best friend
- **LILITH chat:** the AI BFF. She can talk to it any time about anything. It knows her chart, her cycle phase, her recent moods, her recurring characters (the situationship, the boss, the mother). It hypes, it listens, it reality-checks ("girl, take a chill pill, it's not that deep"), it remembers
- Chat memory lives on her device; she can see and delete everything it knows
- Compatibility: add anyone by birth date (time and place optional, degrade gracefully), synastry across love, friendship, work, and family
- Life areas: dedicated tabs for Love, Career, Money, Family showing how current transits hit HER specific houses

### Phase 4 — The world
- **Astrocartography:** map of where on Earth her chart shines, PLUS location-aware mode: with her permission, the app notices when she's traveling and reads the place for her — "You just landed in your Venus line. Rome is obsessed with you. Expect to be perceived"
- Friends: invite friends, see their day at a glance ("Mercury is wrecking her too, be nice")
- Messaging between friends in-app
- Android version

Note on ordering: the chat gets exponentially better with each layer underneath it (chart → cycle → tarot themes), which is why it's Phase 3 and not Phase 1. A BFF who doesn't know your lore yet is just a chatbot.

## Monetization

Freemium with two paid tiers. Chat is both the main value and the main cost, so chat allowance is the tier ladder.

**Free:** daily horoscope, basic chart, moon phase, the daily affirmation (never paywalled — those are the brand), and a taste of chat (a few messages a day, enough to fall in love).

**LILITH+ at $14.99/month or $79.99/year**, 7-day free trial. Everything: full chart deep-dives, weekly and monthly horoscopes, tarot spreads, compatibility, life areas, astrocartography, plus a generous-but-limited chat allowance (start around 25 messages/day; tune with real data). Hitting the limit stays in voice: "LILITH needs her beauty sleep. More tomorrow, or go BFF and we never stop talking."

**LILITH BFF at $24.99/month or $149.99/year:** unlimited chat, longer memory, first access to new features. For the girls whose pocket best friend is genuinely their pocket best friend.

Pricing rationale: $14.99 is premium for astrology apps (CHANI and Co-Star sit lower) but mid-range for an AI companion (ChatGPT Plus is $20). The tier split protects the margin: heavy chatters, the most expensive users, are exactly the ones paying $24.99. Most subscribers will never hit the + limit, so it reads as generosity, not stinginess. Revisit numbers only after retention data exists; the annual plans are where the real margin lives.

## Trust and safety (non-negotiable)

- Cycle predictions are wellness content, NOT contraception or medical advice. Say so in-app
- Hormone content is education about typical patterns, never a claim about HER levels (the app cannot measure anything). The bridge line is mandatory: extreme or persistent symptoms get a warm nudge toward a real doctor, with suggested questions to ask
- Care reminders state their guideline source, are adjustable (guidelines differ by country and personal history), and always say "book it with your provider." The app reminds; it never screens, diagnoses, or reassures away a symptom
- Cycle data is sensitive. Location data is sensitive. Together they are the most sensitive combination an app can hold. Store on-device by default, encrypted, never sold, no ad SDKs ever, deletable in one tap. This is also a marketing point — say it loudly
- The chat is a best friend, not a therapist, and knows the difference: if a conversation shows real crisis signals, LILITH softens the persona, says plainly that this is beyond an app, and surfaces real support resources. Caring honesty IS the brand voice in that moment
- The reality-check rule also protects users: the app never feeds genuinely harmful delusions (about exes, enemies, health, money). Delulu is aspirational confidence, not detachment from safety
- Astrology is framed as reflection and fun, not deterministic instruction for medical, legal, or financial decisions
- Location-aware astrocartography is strictly opt-in, explained in plain language, with When-In-Use permission first

## Success metrics for v1

- Onboarding completion above 70 percent, with the sign-in step's drop-off measured separately — if the account wall costs more than 20 percent of installs, we revisit the wall, not the dream
- Day-7 retention above 25 percent (horoscope apps live and die on the daily open)
- Screenshot shares: the daily card must be designed to be screenshotted and posted
